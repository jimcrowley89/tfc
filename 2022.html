The Terrifying Challenge
2022: The Assault

Scream

Genre: Slasher
Year: 1996
Length: 1h51m
Nihilistic:
Parody?:

Let's start with a hell of a movie: Scream. This one is cool because it plays like a casual teen slasher, but it's also a dense and introspective film. Very aware of its own tropes, Scream plays with genre in a natural and entertaining way.

First off the pacing in this movie is crazy, it flows from kill to conversation without missing a beat. The camera work is distinct and frenetic, which makes the whole thing sort of stand out in your memory. And then there's a whole mess of talented actors (including our first Shaggy of the year). Henry Winkler's in it, Drew Barrymore, Seth Green. It's nuts.

Scream is very polished, very slick, but in a nineties kind of way. There's a lot going on with this one, and I'm psyched to watch it again.

Beware:
You can watch this one as casually as you want. It's just a touch long, but that's not a bad thing.

Psycho

Genre: Mystery / Slasher
Year: 1960
Length: 1h49m
Dry:
Creepy:

Let's talk about Psycho. It's an iconic film, and has a lot of things going for it, but it's also really weird. It's a gritty and surreal sort of movie that's bizarre even by today's standards.

Psycho is incredibly tense, unsettling, and in spots is also pretty brutal. But it really operates more like a mystery than a modern slasher: there's a lot of little details and investigations and characters just talking to each other. Every scene is executed perfectly, but it might not be what you expect from a movie called Psycho.

Also: if you've never seen this movie before, if you don't know the twists and turns it takes, then make sure to avoid spoilers. Go in as fresh as you possibly can (trust me).

Beware:
It helps to approach this one like a mystery rather than a slasher. The first thirty or so minutes are setting up that mystery, so get through that and you'll be golden.

The Crow

Genre: Action / Revenge Film
Year: 1994
Length: 1h42m
Violent:
Nineties Vibe:

This movie is great and also it kicks ass. The Crow is decidedly not a horror film, but it is a great movie for Halloween. It's like a Halloween action film, and I don't know why there aren't more of those.

There's so much to love about this one--great writing, quick pacing, killer soundtrack. Brandon Lee is a national treasure in this film, and the whole cast nails every performance (keep an eye out for some familiar actors).

And finally there's my favorite part: the tone. Though badass and action-packed, The Crow is a tragedy. The whole movie is held together by a constant sadness that's surprisingly fun to watch.

Beware:
The Crow is probably in our "most casual" category this year. It's a chill movie to watch whenever, alone or in a party.

Critters

Genre: Sci-Fi / Horror
Year: 1986
Length: 1h26m
Goofy:
Solid:

Critters is a strange little b-movie. Essentially it's about a bunch of tiny monsters that terrorize a family, but there's more to it than that. It takes a standard knockoff monster movie and adds just enough unique shit to make it interesting.

This movie pretty casual. It's got a nice dash of sci-fi which it uses to good effect, and it doesn't take itself too seriously. There's also some clever little details in the writing that keeps things fresh--and it knows not to overstay its welcome. It's balanced for what it is.

I think this movie has something of a cult following, and I'd say that's deserved. It stands out from other b-movies and sticks with you a little better, too.

Beware:
This one's easy. Just a simple and watchable curiosity.

Child's Play

Genre: Horror
Year: 1988
Length: 1h27m
Iconic:
Funny:

It's not for nothing that Chucky is a horror icon. Child's Play is well-executed, has some cool gimmicks, and borders on horror/comedy. It's pretty great for a killer doll movie.

I'm starting to come around on some of these eighties pop-horror movies. Child's play is interesting in that it has a charismatic monster. Chucky is a homicidal maniac, sure, but he's also kind of funny--and at times nearly sympathetic. It's such a weird situation, but it works.

Quick shoutout to Brad Dourif (the gemini killer in Exorcist III) for carrying this film with his performance of a homicidal doll. It really brings the whole thing together.

Beware:
I really enjoyed this one. Pairs very well with alcohol.

Thir13en Ghosts

Genre: Horror / Action
Year: 2001
Length: 1h31m
Intense:
Novel:

This movie is hard to place in the world of horror. It's got a little bit of action, little bit of mystery, little bit of drama. It's kind of like Cube meets Ghostbusters, if that makes any sense.

Thir13en Ghosts is like a haunted house movie under pressure. It's not a subtle film; the ghosts are more like monsters in their ability to wreck your shit. There's an ominous bodily threat around every corner, and Thir13en Ghosts delivers on that threat.

It's got an interesting cast, and the set design is unique. It's a pretty cool little movie that's been mostly forgotten, I think. Maybe check it out.

Beware:
I'm not sure how to prepare you for this, but it's not too difficult. Seizure warning, because there's some intense strobe effects in this one.

Murder Party

Genre: Horror / Comedy
Year: 2007
Length: 1h19m
Indie:
Clever:

Murder Party is a tiny little independent film with a lot to offer. It's one of those shoestring types, where they somehow turned six actors and a room into a compelling story.

This movie is simple, but it gets a lot out of a straightforward premise. There's humor, and tension, and it moves at a clip. It's also one of those escalating movies that just keeps getting crazier as it goes.

Oh, and it takes place on Halloween night. Murder Party is a great watch for the season, and an interesting piece in general.

Beware:
It takes a little while to get going, but once it does it really takes off.

Malignant

Genre: Horror
Year: 2021
Length: 1h51m
Batshit:
Masterpiece:

If you were going to pick one movie to watch this year, I'd probably recommend Malignant. It's modern classic.

Some movies just have everything you could possibly want out of them. Mystery, suspense, action, intrigue, legitimate horror: it's all there. But at the same time there are elements of parody? It's hard to find anything to compare Malignant against, which I think speaks to its originality.

Perhaps most importantly, this one is nuts. On top of everything else the story is just insane. There are twists and turns and revelations. It's insane.

Beware:
No spoilers. Try and watch this one as fresh as you can.

The Sixth Sense

Genre: Drama
Year: 1999
Length: 1h47m
Iconic:
Somber:

This movie is exactly my type. It's quiet, and thoughtful, and not afraid to get dark. The Sixth Sense combines great execution and an original premise, and it doesn't pull any punches.

If you have never heard of this movie and don't know how it ends, drop what you're doing and go watch it. Because spoilers for this movie are littered everywhere throughout pop culture--which speaks to just how successful it really was. I mean, we all kind of know the twist, right?

Thing is, the memes don't do this movie justice. The film itself is this beautiful supernatural drama that's hard to compare to any other. Haley Joel Osment fucking kills it in this movie dude, and obviously Bruce Willis kicks ass as well. The plot is amazing, even with all the shenanigans aside, and the whole movie is just exceptionally well put-together. The Sixth Sense is a unique piece, and totally worth a watch.

Beware:
This is a sleepy movie. It's very quiet and slow and deliberate. It moves at a good pace, but it's a drama, so plan accordingly.

Bone Tomahawk

Genre: Horror
Year: 2015
Length: 2h12m
Nasty:
Slow:

Here's something you dont see every day: a horror movie set in the old west. It's a great pairing--but damn they went really hard. This movie is black metal.

Picture the grittiest western you can imagine and then add a greasy exploitation film: that's basically Bone Tomahawk. It takes full advantage of the brutal setting, but builds its own horrible mythology on top of that. And seriously, this one's rough. It's rough and fucking weird.

Kurt Russell carries this film, but there are plenty of great performances to go around. The story is strong, the effects are fantastic. Though it's not the most accessible movie ever made I think it's well worth a watch.

Beware:
A fair warning: Bone Tomahawk features one of the most grisly and unpleasant scenes I've ever seen put to film. Be careful not to watch this in mixed company. Also, it's very dry.

Annihilation

Genre: Sci-Fi / Horror
Year: 2018
Length: 1h55m
Bizarre:
Visual:

Annihilation is a weird one. Featuring bits of Lovecraftian, cosmic and body horror alongside a compelling sci-fi premise, it's quite a layered piece.

We've seen a lot of sci-fi/horrors over the years, and it's always cool to see the variety of that genre. Annihilation absolutley has components you've seen before, but there've been unified into a deep and grounded narrative.

But the coolest part of this one is the union between sci-fi and horror. It's a movie that's acutely grounded in reality, which makes the horror even more distinct. There's some really chilling shit in this movie.

Beware:
Annihilation is fairly casual but a little more cerebral. It's also mumbly so keep that remote nearby.

Hellraiser

Genre: Body Horror
Year: 1987
Length: 1h34m
Classic:
Nasty:

Oh, God. Despite how great this movie is I find it hard to watch. It's gross, and more than a little unpleasant. Get ready for some goopy horror.

It's so hard to find good body horror, but Hellraiser is a top-notch title. It's fuckin' gross. Not just in terms of the practical effects, either; just the vibe of the movie is visceral and dirty. It does a great job at capturing this nastiness, while also being just generally good.

Not to mention, at times this film is borderline experimental. It features some pretty shocking imagery, especially in the kickass scenes that feature the Cenobytes. Hellraiser has some pretty unique sights to show you (if you can handle them, that is).

Beware:
This movie is weird and sexy. Don't watch in mixed company.

Psycho II

Genre: Drama / Slasher
Year: 1983
Length: 1h53m
Great Sequel:
Creepy:

Ok, so we're knocking out the entire Psycho franchise this year (as far as I'm concerned). Turns out, someone made a sequel to Psycho decades after the fact, and it's pretty good.

Psycho II is interesting because it pretty well captures the spirit of the original without feeling like a cheap knockoff. Like the original, it's a mystery/slasher, it takes place mostly in the Bates house, and it centers around the character of Norman Bates. It expands on Psycho in an organic way that's surprisingly faithful to its source material.

Every element has strong execution, but I really appreciate the writing especially. The plot is great, but for me it's all the fine details that really bring this movie to life. It may not be a Hitchcock but it's pretty damn good.

Beware:
While the pacing is a little more modern than the original, Psycho II is still on the meditative end of the spectrum. It's a character study as much as a slasher.

Possession

Genre: Psychological / Body Horror
Year: 1981
Length: 2h4m
Dark:
Disturbing:

Despite being a little more under-the-radar than most, Possession is absolutely one of those top-tier masterpieces in league with your Shinings and Chainsaw Massacres. It's dark, it's greasy, and most importantly it's super weird.

And I know it's been a weird year in general, but seriously, this one is something different. Much of Possession is a tense drama, but it's horrific from the very start. You can feel the claustrophobia and paranoia in every scene--and that's before the nightmare shit start showing up. Everything just keeps devolving into delirium and darkness. It's great.

Not to mention the craftwork in this movie, which is insane. Gorgeous camera work, playing with light and depth, borders on experimental without breaking immersion. Great actors turn in some truly wild performances. The special effects are disgustingly believable, so much so that you kind of forget they're effects. I can't recommend it enough.

Beware:
This one would not be fun to drink to. It's more of a dismal nightmare movie so plan accordingly. Oh, and it's grossly sexual too.

House

Genre: Horror / Comedy
Year: 1977
Length: 1h28m
Stylized:
Chaos:

When you're talking about horror/comedy, a good quality to look for is "zaniness." You want to go way over the top, with so much madness and gore that the it becomes funny. And that's House, man. This movie is off the chain from the first frame.

House is so stylized that it's actually kind of shocking at first. It's a haunted house horror movie, shot and framed like an experimental sitcom or something, and operating on cartoon logic. It's also an ensemble/party adventure--like a Ghostbusters, or Lord of the Rings. And did I mention it's Japanese?

I love this movie, it's a one-of-a-kind piece that's assembled brilliantly. Unlike any other I've ever seen.

Beware:
There's a lot going on in this one, and it moves fast. Add subtitles and it can be difficult to keep up.

Tenebrae

Genre: Mystery / Slasher
Year: 1982
Length: 1h41m
Stylish:
Badass:

Tenebrae is a powerhouse of an Italian slasher. To my surprise, it has worked its way onto my list of all-time favorites recently, because it's a perfect film that's also fun to watch.

This movie has a tangible energy to it, kind of like Scream. It feels distinct and unique, even though it is similar to Scream and a lot of other slashers, really. But Tenebrae takes that genre and adds a few twists and turns, covers its tracks. It also captures some fucking unforgettable shots on camera.

If you're just picking one secret film this year, I'd go with this one. It's such an enjoyable experience and a great way to end the season on a high note.

Beware:
No problems here, this one's chill. Happy Halloween.
